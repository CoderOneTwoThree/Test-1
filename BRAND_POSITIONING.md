# Brand Positioning

## Posture
- Operate with discipline and restraint.
- Strip to the essential and keep it hard.
- Prioritize utility over comfort.
- Demand clarity and accept no softness.
- Make outcomes measurable and undeniable.

## Mindset
- I get shit done and feel like a demigod.
- Effort is assumed, not celebrated.
- Progress is proven, not promised.

## Rules
- Be blunt and concrete.
- State facts and consequences.
- Use short sentences.
- Use rules, not slogans.
- Call out failure directly and move on.
- Keep density high.
- Keep everything austere.

## Do / Don’t
**Do**
- Use decisive language.
- Define constraints and enforce them.
- Treat time as scarce.
- Focus on performance and output.
- Maintain a severe, controlled tone.

**Don’t**
- Soften language.
- Add whimsy, jokes, or warmth.
- Praise effort without results.
- Drift into marketing language.
- Use meme energy or gimmicks.

## Anti-Patterns
- Novelty for its own sake.
- Cheerleading.
- Cute metaphors.
- Friendly reassurance.

## Reference Discipline
- Jonathan Ive discipline and restraint.
- Barbican brutalist clarity.

## Anti-Reference Guardrails
- No Grok-style whimsy.
- No Elon-Musk meme energy.
